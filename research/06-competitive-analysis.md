# Competitive Analysis: Mississippi Aggregate Hauling & Dump Truck Companies

> Last updated: February 27, 2026

---

## Overview

Mississippi's aggregate hauling market is fragmented and dominated by small operations. Manta.com lists only **44 dump trucking businesses** across the entire state of Mississippi â€” a remarkably low number for the level of construction activity now underway. This fragmentation, combined with $49 billion in data center construction, creates a clear supply-demand imbalance.

The competitive landscape breaks down into several categories:
1. **Dedicated hauling companies** (pure trucking/hauling operations)
2. **Civil/site work contractors with hauling divisions** (own trucks as part of broader construction services)
3. **Quarry/aggregate producers** (may do some delivery hauling)
4. **Regional/multi-state carriers** (larger operations that touch Mississippi)
5. **Owner-operators and micro-companies** (1-3 trucks)

## Market Gaps Identified

### 1. Dedicated Aggregate Hauling in Jackson Metro -- CRITICAL GAP
There is no large, professional, dedicated aggregate hauling company based in the Jackson metro area. The contractors on data center projects (Birdsong, Southern Rock, Wages Civil, Malouf) all need to HIRE haulers, but the local supply is fragmented among tiny owner-operators and a few small carriers.

### 2. Meridian Market -- WIDE OPEN
With only Davidson Hauling (14 trucks) as the significant local player, the Meridian market is dramatically underserved for the 8-year, $10B Compass project. A satellite operation here would face minimal competition.

### 3. Belly Dump / Road Base Spreading
Most local companies operate standard dump trucks. There is limited belly dump capacity in the market. Data center projects require massive amounts of road base material that is most efficiently placed via belly dump trailers. This is a differentiating equipment advantage.

### 4. Professional Fleet Operations
Most competitors are either construction companies that happen to own some trucks, or small 1-5 truck operations. There is a gap for a company that combines:
- Dedicated hauling focus (not distracted by construction work)
- 5-15 truck capacity (reliable availability)
- Professional dispatch and fleet management
- GPS tracking and load documentation
- Consistent, on-time delivery

### 5. Vicksburg Market -- EMPTY
There is effectively no dedicated aggregate hauling company in Warren County. As the $3B AWS project enters construction in 2026, haulers will need to come from Jackson (40 miles) or emerge locally.

### 6. Multi-Project Capability
No single hauling company currently has the capacity and geographic positioning to serve multiple data center projects simultaneously. A company based in the Jackson metro with a Meridian satellite could serve 4 of 5 projects.

---

## Competitive Positioning Recommendations

### Differentiation Strategy

1. **Dedicated hauler, not a contractor** -- Unlike Birdsong, Hemphill, or Southern Rock, focus exclusively on hauling. This makes you a partner, not a competitor, to the civil contractors.

2. **Belly dump capability** -- Most small operators have standard dump trucks. Belly dump trailers for road base spreading are a clear differentiator, especially for data center pad/road construction.

3. **Reliable multi-truck availability** -- Contractors complain about owner-operators being unreliable. Offering 3-5+ trucks available daily, with professional dispatch, is a major competitive advantage over fragmented small operators.

4. **Jackson metro base with Meridian capability** -- No existing hauler can serve both corridors. This geographic positioning covers $29B of the $49B pipeline.

5. **Technology and professionalism** -- GPS tracking, digital BOLs, prompt invoicing, clean CSA record. Most small competitors operate with paper tickets and cell phone dispatch.

### Who to Partner With (Not Compete Against)

| Partner Type | Companies | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Quarries/Producers | APAC, Rogers Group, Memphis Stone & Gravel | They supply material, you haul it |
| Civil Contractors | Birdsong, Southern Rock, Wages Civil, Malouf | They need haulers for their projects |
| General Contractors | Yates, Gray, Haskell | They award hauling subcontracts |
| Site Services | Mighty Fresh | Complementary services, shared relationships |

### Who to Watch

## Key Takeaways

1. **The market is wide open.** Central Mississippi has almost no dedicated aggregate hauling capacity relative to the $19B+ in active construction. This validates the business plan's core thesis.

2. **Most "competitors" are actually customers.** The civil contractors (Birdsong, Southern Rock, Hemphill, Wages Civil) need to hire haulers. They are the demand side, not the supply side.

3. **V McGee is the only scaled competitor** with 60+ dump trucks, but they are 200+ miles away in DeSoto County and focused on the Memphis metro market.

4. **Meridian is a greenfield opportunity.** Davidson Hauling's 14 trucks are the only meaningful competition for an 8-year, $10B project.

5. **Speed to market matters.** Multiple projects are in heavy earthwork NOW (2026). Every month of delay is lost revenue that can never be recovered.

6. **Belly dump capacity is virtually non-existent** among local haulers. This is a clear equipment differentiator.

7. **Quarry partnerships are critical.** APAC (10 sand/gravel operations, largest MS producer) and Rogers Group are the key relationships. They need contract haulers and can provide steady base load work beyond the data center projects.
