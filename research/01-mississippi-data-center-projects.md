## Detailed Project Profiles

### 1. xAI "MACROHARDRR" — Southaven, DeSoto County

- **Investment:** $20+ billion — largest private investment in Mississippi history
- **Capacity:** Nearly 2 GW upon full completion; currently running temporary turbines generating up to 495 MW on-site
- **Facility Size:** 810,000 sq ft retrofit, plus acquired former Duke Energy power plant site
- **Timeline:** Operations began February 2026
- **Status:** OPERATIONAL / EXPANDING — 27 gas turbines installed, applied for permits for 41 natural gas turbines
- **Power:** Self-generated via on-site natural gas turbines; adjacent to TVA power plant
- **Jobs:** Hundreds of permanent jobs in DeSoto County
- **Controversy:** Significant environmental and permitting issues — Southern Environmental Law Center alleges power plant was built illegally; Feb 2026 public hearing saw overwhelming opposition to proposed permit

### 2. AWS — Madison County (Canton & Ridgeland)

- **Investment:** $10 billion
- **Sites:** 927-acre Madison County Mega Site (Canton) + 786-acre Costas Property (Ridgeland) = ~1,700 acres total
- **Capacity:** ~650 MW; AWS committed to enabling 1.3 GW of new carbon-free energy in MS
- **Timeline:** Announced Jan 2024; construction underway; complete by mid-2027
- **Status:** UNDER CONSTRUCTION — 6,000-7,000 construction workers through 2027
- **Jobs:** 1,000+ permanent at ~$80,000/year average
- **Power:** Entergy building 760 MW natural gas combined-cycle plant in Madison County ($2-3B investment)
- **Contractors:**
  - **General:** W.G. Yates & Sons, Gray Construction, Haskell
  - **Electrical:** Cupertino Electric, MMR Group, Faith Technologies, Edwards Electrics
  - **Site Work:** Birdsong Construction (Clinton, MS), Southern Rock Construction (Brandon, MS), Wages Civil LLC (Canton, MS)
  - **75+ Mississippi companies** currently engaged
- **Incentives ("Project Atlas"):** 10-year 100% corporate income tax exemption, sales/use tax exemptions, 3.15% construction cost rebate, 30-year rolling tax exemption

### 3. Compass Datacenters — Meridian, Lauderdale County

- **Investment:** $10 billion total over full build-out
- **Location:** Meridian I-20/59 Industrial Park, adjacent to I-20 and I-59 corridors
- **Capacity:** 500 MW total campus — eight buildings, each 250,000 sq ft with 40 MW IT capacity
- **Timeline:** Groundbreaking late January 2025; eight buildings over 8-year period
- **Status:** UNDER CONSTRUCTION (early phase)
- **Jobs:** 1,000+ direct and indirect
- **Power:** Mississippi Power supplying ~500 MW
- **Support:** MDA site preparation assistance, $4M state legislature site grant, City of Meridian water/sewer investment

### 4. AVAIO Digital Taurus — Brandon, Rankin County

- **Investment:** $6 billion — third-largest economic development project in state history
- **Location:** East Metropolitan Center Business and Industrial Park
- **Capacity:** Phase 1 delivers 116 MW across 600,000+ sq ft on 170+ acres
- **Timeline:** Broke ground Sept 2025; Phase 1 complete first half 2027
- **Status:** UNDER CONSTRUCTION (Phase 1)
- **Jobs:** 60+ direct high-tech jobs plus hundreds of indirect
- **Connectivity:** Direct access to multiple long-haul fiber routes (Dallas + Atlanta hubs)
- **Sustainability:** Water-efficient cooling, rainwater recapture, rooftop solar

### 5. AWS — Warren County (Vicksburg)

- **Investment:** $3 billion — largest single capital investment in Warren County history
- **Location:** Near Port of Vicksburg, ~40 miles west of Jackson
- **Timeline:** Announced Nov 2025; construction expected to begin 2026
- **Status:** PRE-CONSTRUCTION / ANNOUNCED
- **Jobs:** 200+ full-time data center jobs, 300+ additional community positions, ~1,000 construction workers at peak
- **Power:** Entergy building 754 MW combined-cycle plant at decommissioned Baxter Wilson site ($1.2B)

## Geographic Corridors

### Corridor A: Central Mississippi / Jackson Metro (Highest Density)
- AWS Madison County ($10B) + AVAIO Rankin County ($6B) = **$16B within ~30 miles**
- Centered on Jackson metro; benefits from Entergy grid infrastructure

### Corridor B: Western Mississippi / Vicksburg
- AWS Warren County ($3B)
- I-20 corridor, ~40 miles west of Jackson; Entergy building $1.2B power plant

### Corridor C: Eastern Mississippi / Meridian
- Compass ($10B) at I-20/I-59 interchange
- Mississippi Power (Southern Company); excellent highway connectivity

### Corridor D: Northern Mississippi / Memphis Metro
- xAI ($20B) in Southaven, DeSoto County
- Memphis metro proximity; TVA infrastructure

**Pattern:** The **I-20 corridor from Vicksburg through Jackson to Meridian** is the primary data center belt.

## Infrastructure & Earthwork Demand

- **Land clearing/grading:** Projects range from 175 acres (AVAIO) to 1,700 acres (AWS Madison)
- **Earthwork volumes:** A comparable 122-acre project required 700,000 CY earthwork, 1.5M sq yd chemical soil modification, 100,000 tons aggregate
- **Power infrastructure:** Entergy building two new combined-cycle gas plants totaling 1,500+ MW new generation ($3-4B combined)
- **Road infrastructure:** Heavy-duty access roads for transformer/generator deliveries
- **Water/sewer:** Municipal connections and/or on-site treatment for cooling

## State Incentive Programs

### Data Center Enterprise (DCE) Program
- $20M minimum investment + 20 jobs at 125% state avg wage = 10-year income tax exemption
- $50M minimum + 50 jobs at 150% avg wage = sales/use tax exemption on computing equipment

### Pending Legislation (SB 3168)
- Raises thresholds to $250M for new construction
- Adds electricity tax exemption
- Two automatic 10-year extensions for expansions
- Excludes cryptocurrency mining
